  • In each period, firms set a price (0-100 ECU) for the sale of goods to six consumers.
  • The firm that sets the lowest price sets the market price and serves all consumers.
    The profit is: market price × number of consumers served.
  • The other firms that have chosen a higher price do not make any money.
  • If more than one firm set the lowest price, they divide the consumers among themselves.
  • The consumers are other participants of the experiment. In each period, they receive a new endowment of 100 ECU and must buy one good at the market price.
    Thus, setting higher market prices lowers the payoffs of the consumers.
  • After each period, firms learn the price choices and profits of all firms, their resulting own profit, and consumer expenditures.
  • The experiment consists of at least 20 periods. Once the 20th period is over, the computer will throw a 6-sided virtual dice that will determine whether the experiment continues. If a value of six is shown, the experiment is over. If any other value is shown, the experiment continues.
